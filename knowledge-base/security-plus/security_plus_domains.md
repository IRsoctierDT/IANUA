# CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) — Exam Domains

**Certification:** CompTIA Security+
**Exam version:** SY0-701 (launched November 2023; current as of 2026)
**Authoritative source:** <https://www.comptia.org/en/certifications/security/>

> Security+ is a vendor-neutral, entry-level credential validating baseline skills
> for security roles. The SY0-701 objectives (Exam Objectives v5.0) organize the
> body of knowledge into five weighted domains.

## The five domains (with exam weighting)

| # | Domain | Weight |
|---|--------|-------:|
| 1 | **General Security Concepts** — security controls, CIA triad, zero trust, cryptography basics, change management | 12% |
| 2 | **Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Mitigations** — threat actors, attack surfaces, vulnerability types, mitigation techniques | 22% |
| 3 | **Security Architecture** — secure design across cloud, network, data, and resilience | 18% |
| 4 | **Security Operations** — monitoring, incident response, identity & access, hardening, automation | 28% |
| 5 | **Security Program Management and Oversight** — governance, risk management, compliance, audits, third-party risk | 20% |

Domain 4 (Security Operations) carries the most weight, reflecting the day-to-day
work of SOC and security-engineering roles.

## How this knowledge base uses it

This folder supports certification study **and** practical SOC skill development.
The domains map onto the rest of this knowledge base and the project's agents:

- **Domain 4 (Security Operations)** — the SOC Analyst, Threat Intel, MITRE Mapper,
  and Incident Report agents are direct, hands-on examples of these objectives.
- **Domain 2 (Threats & Vulnerabilities)** — pairs with the MITRE ATT&CK and OWASP
  Top 10 KB entries.
- **Domain 5 (Program Management & Oversight)** — pairs with the NIST CSF 2.0 and
  CIS Controls entries and this repo's governance docs.

> **Version note.** SY0-701 is the active exam in 2026; a successor (SY0-801) is
> expected later. Confirm the current exam and objectives at the source above before
> relying on weightings.
